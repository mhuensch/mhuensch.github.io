Painting

HAHA!  I can't think of a single subject that I am less qualified to write about than "painting".  So, if you are an experienced miniature painter, you should ignore this section entirely.  

I paint my miniatures to a Table Top standard and use basic techniques to achieve these results.  I don't have any education or training as a painter and don't have any notable achievements or skills.  I've painted about 50 miniatures at the time of this writing and most of those I've stripped for repainting.

But what I do have are a very particular set of skills, skills I have acquired over a very long career, skills that ...

... allow me to achieve above average results with minimal effort.  

In short, I'm just like most of you!  There are more of us than there are people like James Wappel or Vince Venturella or any of the other people who inspire me take my painting from shitty to less shitty.  While I respect the time and effort these people put into their craft, I'm not willing to put in that kind of effort.  I'm just a normal guy, with a normal job, with normal friends, who wants to play a normal game of Kingdom Death with decent looking miniatures.  Um ... OK the "normal" thing is mostly bullshit ... but you get the idea.

If I had five minutes to chop down a tree, I’d spend the first three sharpening my axe. – Anonymous Woodsman

What I can offer you is a process.  A process that anyone can follow, step by step, to paint your miniatures to a standard you can be proud of. In short, I'll show you how to substitute tools and technology for the talent and skill you lack.

Everything's a Trade-off

The big advantage of painting recipe I use is that it takes very little talent or skill to pull off something that's worthy of being on your table top.  That's a huge benefit, so there are equally large consequences to adopting my approach:

  • You have to use the right materials and equipment.  If you deviate from what I recommend, you're on your own and you're going to need a higher degree of knowledge/skill.
  • You have to be able to execute a small number of simple techniques.  In short, you'll need a little skill - but having no skill - isn't an option.  Don't worry though, I'll tell you how to get through the necessary skill barriers.
  • This recipe produces a vary particular look that's more grimdark than traditional.  If you're looking for bright and clean colors and lines, this recipe won't work for you.

The Grimdark Death Painting Recipe

This is a very simple recipe I've cribbed from people much smarter and more experienced than I am at miniature painting.  The first time you're doing this, I suggest you follow each link below to it's specific page and "sharpen your axe" by reading up on the specific materials and techniques I use in each step.

Step By Step:

  • Prime miniature in black
  • Sketch value highlights in shades of grey
  • Shade parts you want to have color
  • Line parts you want to stand out in black
  • Light from object source and/or ambient lighting
  • Varnish to protect model during play

Recommended Materials (these are Amazon Associate Links):

  • Aribrush
  • Brushes
  • Inks

Needed Skills:

  • Airbrushing
  • Dry Brushing
  • Reductive
  • Glazing
